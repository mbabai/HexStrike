1. Overview

HexStrike is a multiplayer card-combat game on a hex board, with a focus on simultaneous play.

Each player submits a play composed of two cards, which locks them into a set of actions that will resolve over the course of some number of "beats."

The objective is to force opponents off the island by pushing them far into the abyss, or with no way to return back to the land.

"Beats" are the unit of tempo. The game repeats beat cycles until one winner is declared.

2. Setup

Deck Construction Rules

  • A deck consists of a single character, 4 movement cards, and 12 ability cards
  • No duplicate cards are allowed.
  • Step is required in every deck and cannot be removed.
  • Each deck can contain only one Signature Move (indicated by gold titles).
  • The order of ability cards is fixed. There is no shuffling

Starting State

  1. Place characters on starting land hexes at opposite sides, facing each other.
  2. Players draw 4 movement cards and 4 ability cards as opening hand.

3. Game Flow

The game proceeds in repeating beats. Each beat follows this order:

  1. Refresh Phase
  2. Simultaneous Play Phase
  3. Beat Resolution
  4. End of Beat
  5. Proceed to next beat

4. Beat Phase - Detailed Explanation

4.1 Refresh Phase

At the start of each beat, all players who's character is currently on land AND on the draw to their full hand size: 4 movement cards, and 4 ability cards. Once complete, any cards they have in play are "discarded." Movement cards are put in a pile in front of them, and ability cards go under their deck.

Players who's characters are in the abyss adjacent to a land tile, if they have no cards in their hand, maye draw one movement and one ability card (called a "ledge grab).

4.2 Simultaneous Play Phase

All players without cards in play simultaneously choose one active card, one opposite-type passive card, and one legal rotation.

If a player has no cards left in their hand to play at this point, and they are over the Abyss, they lose.

  • Card types are movement (green background) and ability (purple background).
  • Rotations are 0 (forward), L1, L2, R1, R2 (left and right by 1 or 2), or 3 (turn around), and may be limited by the card.

4.3 Beat Resolution

  • For any cards that were just played, immediately resolve the rotation chosen.
  • After that, entries resolve by priority from highest to lowest.
  • If a player is hit with knockback (see knockback rules) before their action resolves, That action is skipped, and their cards are immediately discarded.

4.4 End of Beat

The timeline advances to the next beat for all players' in-play cards.

5. Combat & Resolution Details

Hit Resolution

When a character is hit, resolve as follows:

  • If the defender was blocking, then the attack does nothing
  • If the attack is of type "throw", it cannot be blocked, and instead of regular knockback, the attack chooses to move the defending 2 hexes in any of the 6 directions.
  • If there was no block and it was not a throw, proceed with normal damage and knockback resolution below.

Damage

  • Damage is first added to the target's accumulated damage.
  • There is no limit to how much damage can be accumulated.

Knockback

In HexStrike, the object of the game is knock players off the island. The more damage they have accumulated, the easier it is to knock them off. Knockback Factor ( or KBF) is the factor by which you increase the knocback relative to accumulated damage. The formula is below:

  • Knockback is evaluated after damage is added.
  • KBF = 0 gives 0 knockback.
  • KBF = 1 gives 1 hex knockback.
  • KBF > 1 does KBF * Damage / 10 knockback (rounded down).
  • Knockback direction is always in a straight line away from the attack, in the direction of the defender.
  • If an obstacle is in the way of knockback, knockback stops at that obstacle.
  • Character are stunned for + 1 calculated knockback distance number of beats. After that, they refresh. Obstacles reduce the stun beats as well.
  • If a character is 5 or more hexes away from the island edge, they lose the game.

6. Cards in Hand Economy

Throughout the game, the "number of cards" in players' hands will be referenced frequently. This always refers to non-movement ability cards.

These rules maintain parity between ability and movement cards. Movement cards (indicated with a green background) should always attempt to match the number of non-movement cards in hand.

This means:

  • If the player has 4 or fewer ability cards in hand and must discard, the player must also discard a movement card.
  • If the player has 3 or fewer cards in hand and draws a card, they must also pick up an equivalent number of movement cards of their choosing from their movement discard pile.
  • If the player has 4 or more cards in hand and their active card expires, they must also pick up all movement cards from their discard pile.
  • When an active card is discarded, always discard the passive card with it at the same time.
  • Movement cards go to the movement discard pile.
  • Ability cards go under the ability card deck.

7. Trigger Text

Some cards will have "trigger text" on them. This trigger text takes priority above all other effects, if used.

Example: Burning Strike

"If Burning Strike is in your hand when you land an attack, you may reveal it to discard it and a movement card to place a fire hex on all hexes attacked."

8. Tokens

Tokens are board objects placed by card effects. They persist and resolve according to their own rules.

Arrow Token

Arrow tokens charge () forward in the direction it faces on every beat, dealing 4 damage with a KBF of 1. If it hits a target or is blocked, it leave the board.

Fire Token

Fire tokens sit on land hexes and deal 1 damage per beat to the occupant.

Ethereal Platform

Ethereal Platforms can be placed on abyss hexes. If a character stands on it during a , they can refresh as though on land. Once they do, it vanishes.

9. End of Game

In a 1v1, 2 player game, the game ends immediately once one player loses. A player can lose in one of two ways:

  • If a character is 5 or more hexes away from the island edge, they lose the game.
  • If a player is on abyss at the earliest open beat with no playable cards and no valid ledge grab, they lose the game.

In a Free For All game, (3 player or 4 player), when one player falls off, the game is not immediately over.

  • The player who hit that player last gets a "victory point."
  • The player who fell off discards their hand, and remains out of the game for 10 beats
  • After 10 beats, they are put on the center hext of the island. If it is occupied, pick a free hex adjacent to the center.
  • After being respawned, they draw to full hand, are immune to damage, stun, and knockback for 5 beats, and can play on the next beat.
  • First player to 2 victory points wins the game.

11. Quick Reference

Beat Order

  1. Refresh
  2. Simultaneous Play
  3. Beat Resolution
  4. End of Beat
  5. Proceed to next beat

Damage / Knockback

  • Apply damage first.
  • KBF = 0: 0 knockback.
  • KBF = 1: 1 hex knockback.
  • KBF > 1: max(1, floor((accumulatedDamage * KBF) / 10)).
  • Throw rules replace normal knockback formula.

Deck Restrictions

  • 16 cards total (4 movement, 12 ability).
  • No duplicates.
  • Step is required.
  • Max 1 Signature Move.

Win Condition

  • Opponent is more than 4 hexes from nearest land, or
  • Opponent is on abyss at earliest open beat with no playable cards and no ledge grab.
