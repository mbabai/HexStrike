1. Overview

HexStrike is a simultaneous, deterministic combat game played on a hex grid. Players simultaneously commit an action set by submitting two cards—one active and one passive—plus a legal rotation. That commitment locks in a sequence of actions that will resolve in order across upcoming "beats", units of game tempo. Your objective is to eliminate opponents by forcing them off the island: either knock them deep into the abyss or pressure them into a state where they cannot safely return to land.

2. Setup

Deck Construction Rules

  • Each deck contains: 1 character, 4 movement cards, and 12 ability cards.
  • No duplicate cards are allowed.
  • Step is mandatory and cannot be removed.
  • Each deck may include up to only one Signature Move (gold title).
  • Ability card order is fixed. There is no shuffling.

Starting State

  1. Place characters on designated starting land hexes, facing each other.
  2. Each player draws 4 movement cards and 4 ability cards.

3. Game Flow

The game proceeds in repeating beats. Each beat follows this sequence:

  1. Refresh Phase
  2. Simultaneous Play Phase
  3. Beat Resolution
  4. End of Beat
  5. Advance to Next Beat

4. Beat Phase - Detailed Explanation

4.1 Refresh Phase

At the start of each beat, a player refreshes if their character is on a beat, or has no active card.

To refresh:

  • Discard any active and passive cards.
  • If the player is on land, draw until holding 4 ability cards.
  • Sync movement cards according to the hand economy rules.

Players in the abyss adjacent to land who have no cards in hand may perform a "ledge grab": draw 1 movement and 1 ability card.

4.2 Simultaneous Play Phase

All players without cards in play simultaneously select:

  • 1 active card
  • 1 passive card of the opposite type (movement card vs. ability card)
  • 1 legal rotation

If a player cannot legally play and is over the abyss, they lose immediately.

4.3 Beat Resolution

  • Resolve chosen rotations immediately.
  • Resolve actions by timing: first, then , then .
  • When actions share the same timing on the same beat, use the tie-break rules in Section 12.
  • If a character is knocked back before their action resolves, their action is canceled and their cards are discarded.

4.4 End of Beat

Advance all in-play cards to the next beat on their timeline.

5. Play Details

Every play commits exactly one active card and one passive card from opposite sides (movement + ability). The active card provides the action timeline and rotation limit; the passive card adds its passive text modifier while that action set is in play.

5.1 Active and Passive Parts on Cards

The green-highlighted row is the card's Active text. The blue-highlighted row is the card's Passive text.

5.2 How They Fit in Play Modal

In the play modal, your selected cards are combined into one action set: active text drives the sequence, passive text modifies it, and rotation is locked at submission.

5.3 Rotations

  • Choose one legal rotation when submitting: 0, R1, R2, 3, L2, or L1.
  • The active card's rotation badge (for example ) limits which choices are legal.
  • The selected rotation applies before action resolution and only to the action-set start.

6. Combat & Resolution Details

Hit Resolution

  • If the defender blocks correctly, the attack has no effect.
  • If the attack is a throw, it cannot be blocked and moves the defender exactly 2 hexes in any direction chosen by the attacker.
  • Otherwise, apply damage and knockback.

Damage

Damage is added to the target’s accumulated damage. There is no upper limit.

Knockback

After damage is applied, calculate knockback using the Knockback Factor .

  • KBF = 0: 0 hexes.
  • KBF = 1: 1 hex.
  • KBF > 1: floor((Damage × KBF) / 10), minimum 1.

Knockback moves the defender directly away from the attacker. Obstacles stop movement early.

The defender is stunned for the same number of beats as the knockback distance + 1.

If a character ends 5 or more hexes from the island edge, they lose.

7. Cards in Hand Economy

“Cards in hand” always refers to ability cards only.

Movement cards must always mirror the number of ability cards in hand.

  • If you discard ability cards, discard movement cards to match.
  • If you draw ability cards while at 3 or fewer, recover movement cards from discard to match.
  • If an active card expires while holding 4 or more ability cards, recover all movement cards.
  • Active and passive cards are always discarded together.

8. Trigger Text

Trigger text overrides standard resolution order when its condition is met.

Example – Burning Strike:

"If Burning Strike is in your hand when you land an attack, you may reveal it and discard it along with a movement card to place a fire hex on all attacked hexes."

9. Hexes & Tokens

Hexes define terrain and tokens are board objects placed by card effects. Both persist and resolve by their rules.

Land Hex

Land hexes form the playable island. Land is where normal refreshes occur.

Abyss Hex

Abyss hexes surround the island. Characters pushed too far into the abyss lose.

Arrow Token

Arrow tokens charge () forward in the direction it faces on every beat, dealing 4 damage with a KBF of 1. If it hits a target or is blocked, it leave the board.

Fire Token

Fire tokens sit on land hexes and deal 1 damage per beat to the occupant.

Ethereal Platform

Ethereal Platforms can be placed on abyss hexes. If a character stands on it during a , they can refresh as though on land. Once they do, it vanishes.

10. End of Game

In 1v1 play, the game ends immediately when one player loses.

  • A character is 5 or more hexes from the island edge.
  • A player is on the abyss at the earliest open beat with no playable cards and no ledge grab available.

In Free‑For‑All games, eliminated players respawn after 10 beats in the center hex of the island with 0 damage and a full hand. For the first 5 beats after respawning, They cannot be knocked back, stunned, or dealt damage. The last player to hit someone that is eliminated gets a victory point. First to 2 victory points wins.

12. Tie Breakers

Same Beat + Same Timing

  1. resolves first, then .
  2. Throws resolve before blocks.
  3. Blocks resolve before other non-throw actions.
  4. Attacks resolve next.
  5. Moves resolve next.
  6. resolves before / .
  7. / are lowest.

Within the Same Class

  1. If tied actions are from different points in each action set, the later step resolves first (for example, step 3 beats step 2).
  2. If two attacks still tie, higher KBF resolves first.
  3. If two attacks still tie on KBF, both hit. IF they are striking a third target, apply damage from both hits first, then apply only one knockback chosen by the target from the available hit directions.
  4. If two moves still tie and both try to enter the same hex, both moves fail (treated as blocked movement).
  5. If two / choices still tie, those choices are made simultaneously.

13. Quick Reference

Beat Order

  1. Refresh
  2. Simultaneous Play
  3. Beat Resolution
  4. End of Beat
  5. Proceed to next beat

Damage / Knockback

  • Apply damage first.
  • KBF = 0: 0 knockback.
  • KBF = 1: 1 hex knockback.
  • KBF > 1: max(1, floor((accumulatedDamage * KBF) / 10)).
  • Throw rules replace normal knockback formula.

Deck Restrictions

  • 1 character, 16 cards total (4 movement, 12 ability).
  • No duplicates.
  • Step is required.
  • Max 1 Signature Move.

Win Condition

  • Opponent is more than 4 hexes from nearest land, or
  • Opponent is on abyss at earliest open beat with no playable cards and no ledge grab.
  • FFA: 2 victory points wins: get victory points by "last hitting" opponents.
