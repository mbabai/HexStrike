1) Game Overview

HexStrike is a two-player, timeline-based card combat game on a hex board. You are not choosing one action at a time. Instead, you lock in a paired card plan that can span multiple beats, then the server resolves both players' plans in a shared timeline.

Objective

Win by forcing the opponent to lose. A player loses if either condition is met:

  • A player is more than 4 hex tiles away from land at any point.
  • A player has no more cards to play when it is their turn to play a card.

Core Components

  • Hex board with central land and surrounding abyss.
  • Two characters with facing direction and accumulated damage.
  • Per-player deck package: four movement cards and twelve ability cards.
  • Shared beat timeline with one entry per player per beat.
  • Interaction pauses for throws, discard prompts, combo prompts, and hand-trigger prompts.

2) Setup

  1. Each player picks one character and one deck (4 movement, 12 ability, no duplicates).
  2. Characters spawn on opposite sides of land, facing each other.
  3. All movement cards start available (not exhausted).
  4. Draw ability cards up to max hand size (normally 4, modified by character/focus effects).

3) Reading a Card

Every submission uses exactly two cards of opposite types: one movement card and one ability card. One is designated active, the other passive. The active card supplies the action timeline and base stats. The passive card modifies behavior through passive text.

Card Anatomy

  • Left vertical symbol lane: beat-by-beat action sequence for the active card.
  • Top title bar: card name.
  • Upper-right badges: priority and rotation allowance.
  • Middle text area: active text instructions.
  • Lower text area: passive text instructions.
  • Bottom-left badges on ability cards: damage and knockback factor.

Active vs Passive Role

  • The active card puts its symbol sequence onto your timeline.
  • Active card determines base priority, damage, and knockback factor for attacks.
  • Passive card modifies the active list and/or adds conditional effects.
  • You cannot pick two cards of the same type in one submission.

4) Cards in Hand and Card Economy

In HexStrike rules text, "cards in hand" refers to ability cards unless a rule explicitly says movement. Movement hand size is kept in line with ability hand size using the hand-cap rules below.

Hand Parity Rule

Movement hand attempts to match ability hand up to the player's current maximum hand size. Default max is four, with character and focus effects able to modify it.

Ability Cards in Hand Target Movement Cards in Hand
0 0
1 1
2 2
3 3
4 or more 4 (practical cap from total movement cards)

Draw and Discard Behavior

  • Draw effects add ability cards first, then movement cards are adjusted to match.
  • Discard effects remove ability cards first, then movement cards are adjusted to match.
  • If a discard requirement exceeds current hand size, all available valid cards are used.

Card Zones and Consumption

  • Ability cards cycle through ability hand and ability deck.
  • Movement cards cycle between ready and exhausted states.
  • When a submission is accepted, both selected cards are spent immediately.
  • The selected ability card is removed from hand at submission time and placed under the ability deck, except where focus rules temporarily set it aside.
  • The selected movement card is either exhausted or stays ready depending on current hand-size state, then sync rules apply.

5) Frame Phases

HexStrike can be taught as a repeating five-phase loop: Refresh, Play, Execute, Shift, Repeat. Internally, this means checking who can refresh, locking in player choices, resolving actions in order, and moving to the next beat.

Teaching Version: How a Frame Feels

Refresh

If you can refresh, refill toward max and fix movement-to-ability matching.

Play

Players who are ready choose movement + ability + rotation, then lock it in.

Execute

Actions resolve by priority and symbol meaning, with pauses when players must choose.

Shift

The game advances to the next open beat.

Repeat

The loop starts again from that new beat.

Formal Version: Phase Rules

  1. Refresh phase: Refresh can happen only at the current open beat and only on land (or a valid platform effect). Refresh is blocked while choices are pending or while focus is active.
  2. Play phase: Each ready player picks one movement card, one ability card, and one legal rotation. If both players must act on the same beat, both choices are collected before reveal.
  3. Execute phase: The server resolves by priority, applies rotation first, processes symbols in order, and pauses if input is required.
  4. Shift phase: Resolved history advances and the next open beat becomes active.
  5. Repeat phase: The same loop runs again from the updated game state until a match outcome is reached.

6) Timeline and Submission

Open Beats and Frontier

  • is an open beat.
  • is also treated as open for action insertion and gating in focus rules.
  • The earliest open beat across all players is where new plays happen.
  • The game checks forward from current progress, not from beat zero.

Submission Flow

  1. At your next open beat, choose one active card, one passive card, and one legal rotation.
  2. Your active timeline is inserted at your first open slot without shifting other players' rows.
  3. Selected rotation is attached to the first inserted entry unless later forced by card text.
  4. Old future entries that no longer fit are cleared, while locked future starts are kept.
  5. If both players are required at the same beat, all required submissions are collected first.

Beat Resolution Order

  • Entries resolve by priority (higher first). Ties use roster order.
  • Rotation is applied before token execution.
  • Multi-token actions (for example ) resolve left to right.
  • Each token uses actor state seeded at that beat's execution window.

7) Symbol Resolution and Combat

Movement vs Jump (Important)

  • moves step-by-step and stops early if another character occupies the path.
  • jumps to destination; intermediate occupancy is ignored, but landing on another character is not allowed.

Path Geometry

  • Path directions are relative to facing, not absolute board direction.
  • Distance variants extend travel or reach along the same relative direction.
  • Composite symbols can include multiple attack vectors on the same beat.

Attack and Charge

  • attacks destination hex of its path.
  • is a charge and performs both attack behavior and movement behavior on the same token.

Blocks

  • places a directional block on your current hex.
  • Blocks stop matching attack directions that arrive later in the beat.
  • Throws ignore normal block stopping rules.

8) Knockback and Damage Resolution

Damage Model

  • Damage is accumulated over match time and is not automatically reset during play.
  • Per-hit value is read from active card stats plus active modifiers.
  • Per-hit value is read from active card stats plus active modifiers.

Knockback Formula

X add X to the accumulated damage of the target, first.
0 → knockback 0
1 → knockback 1
2+ → knockback = max(1, floor((accumulated damage x factor) / 10))
T → throw rules replace this formula.

Stun Window and Timeline Rewrite

  • When knockback or stun conditions are met, the defender timeline is rewritten with entries and then re-opened by an open beat marker.
  • Greyed hit markers mean stun timing with no movement knockback.
  • Hit rewrites try not to erase already-locked future actions.
  • Damage and knockback overlays shown on timeline come from per-beat consequences data.

9) Advanced Interactions

Throws

  • When an attack is a throw, instead of calculating knockback, the thrower chooses the knockback direction, and distance is always 2 regardless of accumulated damage.
  • Throw-enabled attacks pause execution and request that direction choice.
  • Throw hits are marked by the knockback badge with and value T.
  • Throw immunity passives block incoming throw resolution windows.

Combo Windows

Combo checks are represented by . A combo prompt opens only when the matching active card landed a valid non-throw hit and combo availability is still true for that sequence.

Focus

  • When appears on the timeline, that card becomes a persistent passive effect until focus is broken.
  • If the focused character is stunned or knocked back, the focus effect ends immediately.
  • When focus ends, the focused card is returned to the bottom of that player's ability deck.

Hand Trigger Prompts

  • Some cards can be revealed from hand when trigger conditions are met.
  • These prompts may require discard payment before effect resolution is applied.
  • Only the globally earliest pending hand-trigger prompt is interactive at a time.

Board Tokens

  • Fire token: applies periodic damage to occupants unless immunity applies.
  • Arrow token: advances each beat and always resolves as with and .
  • Ethereal platform token: can allow land-style refresh over abyss, then is consumed.
  • Focus anchor token: marks active focus anchor positions for visibility.

10) End of Match and Victory

  • Distance loss: if a character becomes more than 4 hex tiles from land, that player loses.
  • No-cards loss: if it is your turn to play and you cannot play a card, you lose.
  • Players may offer a draw during the match.
  • Players may forfeit during the match.

11) Rules Glossary

Ability Card
Non-movement card type. Ability hand count is the primary hand-count reference in rules.
Movement Card
Movement card type that synchronizes to ability hand count through parity rules.
Active Card
Selected card whose action timeline is inserted and whose priority and base stats drive resolution.
Passive Card
Selected companion card whose passive text modifies the active sequence.
Action Set
The active and passive cards plus rotation that you submit for your next open beat.
Beat (Frame)
One timeline index containing one entry per player.
Frontier
The first open beat where new plays are made.
Priority
Resolution order ranking on active cards; higher resolves first.
Rotation
Facing change in sixty-degree increments applied before action resolution.
Accumulated Damage
Total damage on a character used by knockback calculations and card conditions.
Refresh
Refilling your hand on land when game state allows it.
Discard
Card removal action that moves ability cards through ability cycle and syncs movement parity.
Draw
Ability card draw followed by movement parity synchronization.
Throw
Interaction-based hit type that requests direction input and resolves through throw rules.
Touching
Same hex or one of six adjacent hexes.
Land and Abyss
Terrain categories used for refresh eligibility and distance-loss checks.
Combo Window
A choice point where a combo can continue or stop.
Focus
Concentration-like state that can reduce hand cap and modify refresh/return behavior.

13) Quick Reference

Frame Order

  1. Refresh (if allowed on your current open beat)
  2. Play (select movement, ability, and legal rotation)
  3. Execute (resolve by priority and symbol rules)
  4. Shift (move to the next open beat)
  5. Repeat

Loss Conditions

  • More than 4 hex tiles from land.
  • No card available when it is your turn to play.

Knockback Summary

  • Zero factor: no knockback.
  • One factor: one hex knockback.
  • Higher factors: damage-scaled knockback with minimum floor.
